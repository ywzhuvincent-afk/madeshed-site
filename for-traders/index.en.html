You are not the same
trader every day

Same strategy, same market. On some days you follow the plan. On others you chase, you hold a loser, you add to the position you swore you wouldn't.

You already know this. What you can't say is — which days.

How most people handle it

By feel. "Not feeling sharp today, I'll keep it small."

The trouble is that feeling sharp is one of the least reliable signals in trading. A winning streak makes you feel good, and that is precisely when overtrading peaks. Feel lags results. It doesn't lead them.

The other approach is a trade journal. That's right as far as it goes, but most journals record what you did and not what state you were in. So at review time you see "this one lost" instead of "I always lose on days like this."

What Madeshed does

It attaches a time-dimension tag to each entry in your log, then uses your own sample to test one thing: sliced along that dimension, does your win rate actually differ?

The tag comes from BaZi — the relationship between the sexagenary pillars of your birth and those of each day. Why that? Because it gives a periodic index that is independent of the market and fixed at your birth. It can't be contaminated by your recent P&L the way a gut feeling can.

Whether it works for you is decided by your data, not by us. After 30–90 days the system tells you what it found — including "no significant difference," if that's the answer.

Three steps, under a minute a day

1

Generate and save your chart

Enter birth date, time and city. True solar time correction is applied automatically (longitude, DST, equation of time) — a step most calculators skip. Generate once, used site-wide.

2

Log honestly, every day

Record the real outcome: win / flat / loss, and the size. Mark the days you didn't trade too. Honesty is the only requirement — patterns computed from flattered data are worth nothing to you.

3

Read the backtest after 30–90 days

Once the sample is large enough, the system groups your own records: win rate, average P&L and max drawdown under each state. If there's a difference, you'll see it. If there isn't, you'll see that too.

What it explicitly does not do

These four are built into the product, not legal boilerplate

  • It does not forecast prices and recommends no instruments. It holds no view on the market at all.
  • Green does not mean size up or add leverage. Green is a favourable window; position sizing still follows your own risk rules.
  • It does not replace stops or money management. It's an addition, not a safety net.
  • No conclusions on a thin sample. Under 30 days the statistics mean nothing, and the system says so plainly.

If a tool claims it will raise your returns, you should be suspicious of it. Madeshed claims exactly one thing: it can help you see your own behavioural pattern. Everything after that still rests on your strategy and your discipline.

Who it's for

✓ A good fit

  • You already have a trading system; execution discipline is the gap
  • You know you trade emotionally and want to find the trigger
  • You'll spend a minute a day recording honestly
  • You can accept "my data doesn't support this"

✗ Not a fit

  • You want entry signals or stock tips
  • You expect a higher win rate without changing behaviour
  • You'd rather not write down the losses
  • You want it instead of risk management and stops

Why it's worth trying

The cost is a minute a day and a month or two of patience.

What you get back is something nobody else can hand you — a map of your own trading rhythm. Not general advice, not "trend followers should do X," but a statistical conclusion about you, drawn from your own record.

And if the answer turns out to be "no significant difference," you still walk away with something worth having: one variable ruled out, and two months of rigorously kept trade logs.
